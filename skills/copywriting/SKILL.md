Complete system for writing copy that converts — from first draft to scoring. Optimised for tech products, entertainment, and motivation/self-improvement.

## Step 1: Before Writing Anything — Awareness Level

**Every reader sits at one of 5 levels. Your copy must match where THEY are, not where you think they are.**

| Level | They know... | Your job | Lead with |
|-------|-------------|----------|-----------|
| **Unaware** | Nothing about the problem | Make them feel the problem | Story or pattern interrupt |
| **Problem-aware** | They have a problem | Show a solution exists | Empathy + "there's a better way" |
| **Solution-aware** | Solutions exist | Why YOUR solution | Differentiation + proof |
| **Product-aware** | Your product | Why NOW | Offer + urgency + risk reversal |
| **Most-aware** | Everything | Just the deal | Price + CTA + deadline |

**Most copy fails because it's written for the wrong awareness level.**

For **tech products**: audience is usually Solution-aware or Product-aware — they've Googled alternatives.
For **entertainment**: usually Unaware or Problem-aware — they don't know they need what you're offering yet.
For **motivation**: usually Problem-aware — they know they're stuck but don't know your specific approach.

## Step 2: Research (Do This Before Writing)

### Voice-of-Customer Brief

Fill this before writing a single word:

```yaml
product: "[Product name]"
audience:
  who: "[Specific person, not a demographic]"
  current_state: "[What their life/work looks like right now]"
  desired_state: "[What they wish it looked like]"
  trigger_event: "[What makes them search for a solution TODAY]"

messaging:
  primary_desire: "[The one outcome they'd pay anything for]"
  top_objections:
    - "[Their #1 concern — in their words]"
    - "[Their #2 concern]"
    - "[Their #3 concern]"
  proof_they_need: "[What evidence would convince them]"
  words_they_use: ["[Exact phrases from real reviews/forums]"]
  words_to_avoid: ["[Jargon they hate]"]

awareness_level: "[Unaware / Problem-aware / Solution-aware / Product-aware / Most-aware]"
```

### Where to find their real language
- Reddit/forums in your niche — problems described in their own words
- App Store / G2 / Trustpilot reviews of competitors — exact phrases
- Support chat logs — questions they actually ask
- Social media comments on competitor posts — complaints and wishes

## Step 3: Headlines

**The headline does 80% of the work. Write 10+, use 1.**

### 8 Formulas That Work

| # | Formula | Example |
|---|---------|---------|
| 1 | **How to [outcome] without [pain]** | "How to build an audience without posting every day" |
| 2 | **[Number] [things] that [benefit]** | "7 habits that actually stick after day 3" |
| 3 | **Stop [mistake]. Start [better thing].** | "Stop guessing your pricing. Start using data." |
| 4 | **[Specific result] in [timeframe]** | "First paying customer in 14 days" |
| 5 | **Why [common belief] is wrong** | "Why more content is killing your conversion rate" |
| 6 | **[Do thing] like [aspirational identity]** | "Build apps like a senior engineer" |
| 7 | **The [adjective] way to [outcome]** | "The laziest way to build a profitable newsletter" |
| 8 | **What [group they respect] know about [topic]** | "What top creators know about retention that you don't" |

### Industry-Specific Headline Angles

**Tech products:**
- Lead with the metric that matters: speed, uptime, cost saved, time saved
- Be specific: "Deploy in 3 seconds" > "Deploy fast"
- Comparison: "Everything Notion does. Half the price."

**Entertainment:**
- Emotion first: "Never bored on a Sunday again"
- FOMO: "10,000 people watching right now"
- Identity: "For people who take their [hobby] seriously"

**Motivation/self-improvement:**
- Transformation: "From stuck to shipping in 30 days"
- Specificity: "Lose the first 10kg without counting calories"
- Anti-hype: "No morning routines required" (pattern interrupt)

### Headline Quality Score

Rate 1–5 on each. Total ≥ 20 = publish. Below 15 = rewrite.

| Dimension | 1 (Weak) | 5 (Strong) |
|-----------|----------|------------|
| **Specificity** | Vague promise | Exact number or result |
| **Curiosity** | Predictable | "I need to read more" |
| **Relevance** | Generic | Speaks to my exact situation |
| **Clarity** | Confusing | Understood in 2 seconds |
| **Urgency** | "Whenever" | "I need this now" |

## Step 4: Core Frameworks

Choose one per page. Match to awareness level and product type.

### PAS — Problem, Agitation, Solution
**Best for:** Tech products, email, ads. Audience is problem-aware.

```
PROBLEM: Name the specific pain they feel right now.
AGITATION: What happens if they don't fix it? What is it costing them?
SOLUTION: Your product as the relief.
```

**Tech example:**
> Your team spends 3 hours a day on manual data entry. (Problem)
>
> That's 15 hours a week per person — meanwhile your competitors automated this last quarter. Every day you wait, the gap widens. (Agitation)
>
> SalesFlow syncs your CRM in real-time. Zero manual entry. Your team sells; the system handles the rest. (Solution)

---

### AIDA — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action
**Best for:** Landing pages, sales pages. Any awareness level.

| Stage | Job | Technique |
|-------|-----|-----------|
| **Attention** | Stop the scroll | Bold claim, surprising stat, pattern interrupt |
| **Interest** | Keep reading | Story, empathy, "me too" moment |
| **Desire** | Make them want it | Benefits, proof, future pacing |
| **Action** | Get the click | Clear CTA, urgency, risk reversal |

Page structure maps to AIDA: Hero (A+I) → Features (D) → Proof (D) → CTA (A)

---

### BAB — Before, After, Bridge
**Best for:** Motivation and entertainment products. Transformation-focused copy.

```
BEFORE: Paint their current painful reality in specific detail.
AFTER: Show the transformed state they want. Be vivid.
BRIDGE: Your product is the bridge between the two.
```

**Motivation example:**
> You start every Monday with a plan. By Thursday, it's abandoned. You're not lazy — you're using a system designed for someone else. (Before)
>
> Imagine closing your laptop on Friday knowing you shipped the thing you said you would. Every week. (After)
>
> [Product] is a 30-day system built around how you actually work — not how productivity gurus say you should. (Bridge)

---

### PASTOR — Problem, Amplify, Story, Testimony, Offer, Response
**Best for:** Long-form sales pages, high-consideration products, premium pricing.

```
Problem: Name it in their words
Amplify: Emotional + financial cost of inaction
Story: Your journey through the same problem (or a customer's)
Testimony: Social proof from people like them
Offer: Present the solution with clear value
Response: Exact next step
```

---

### 4U Framework
**Best for:** Headlines, subject lines, CTAs. Score each 1-4.

- **Urgent** — Is there a reason to act now?
- **Unique** — Is this different from everything else?
- **Ultra-specific** — Is there a precise number/detail?
- **Useful** — Does it deliver real value?

Target: 12+/16. Below 8 = rewrite.

## Step 5: Feature → Benefit → "So What" Chain

**Never stop at the feature. Go 3 levels deep every time.**

| Level | Question | Example |
|-------|----------|---------|
| **Feature** | What is it? | "Offline mode" |
| **Benefit** | What does it do? | "Works without internet" |
| **So What** | Why does that matter? | "Keep writing on a plane, in a cabin, anywhere — your flow never breaks" |

Always write at the "So What" level. Features describe the product. So What describes the life.

## Step 6: Persuasion & Psychology

### Social Proof Hierarchy
Most persuasive → least:

1. **Specific results with numbers** — "$47K in 30 days" beats "great results"
2. **Named testimonials with photo** — Real person > anonymous quote
3. **Logos of recognisable companies** — "Trusted by Stripe, Notion, Linear"
4. **Numbers at scale** — "12,847 teams" (odd numbers feel more real than round ones)
5. **Media mentions** — "Featured in TechCrunch"
6. **Certifications / awards** — G2 badges, SOC2
7. **Generic quotes** — "This is amazing!" (weakest — avoid if possible)

**For entertainment:** transformation stories from real users > logos. People want to see people like them.
**For motivation:** before/after stories > stats. Emotion beats data.
**For tech:** uptime numbers, security certs, and recognisable company logos > testimonials.

### Objection Handling

| Objection | Copy technique | Example |
|-----------|---------------|---------|
| "Too expensive" | Reframe as cost of inaction | "A single missed deal costs more than a year of this tool" |
| "Don't have time" | Show time saved | "Setup takes 7 minutes. Most users save 2 hours/week by day 3" |
| "Already have something" | Low switching cost | "Import everything in one click. Free migration included." |
| "Not sure it works" | Risk reversal + proof | "60-day guarantee. Here's what [similar person] achieved." |
| "Need to think about it" | Urgency + future cost | "Every week you wait is another week of [the problem]." |
| "Too complicated" | Simplicity proof | "Set up in 5 minutes. No IT needed." |

### Behavioral Psychology Quick Reference

| Situation | Apply these principles |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Page not converting | Loss Aversion, Social Proof, Anchoring, Simplify choices |
| Pricing page | Decoy Effect, Anchoring (show higher price first), Charm Pricing ($49 not $50), Rule of 100 (% off under $100, $ off over $100) |
| Low email signups | Reciprocity (give free value first), Endowment Effect (free trial) |
| High bounce rate | Awareness level mismatch — copy assumes too much |
| CTA not getting clicks | Loss Aversion framing ("Don't miss" > "Get"), first-person text ("Start my free trial") |
| No urgency | Only add urgency if REAL — fake urgency destroys trust permanently |

### Power Words by Emotion

| Emotion | Words |
|---------|-------|
| **Urgency** | Now, today, limited, deadline, before, expires, last chance |
| **Trust** | Guaranteed, proven, secure, certified, verified, backed |
| **Curiosity** | Secret, hidden, little-known, surprising, discover, reveal |
| **Achievement** | Master, unlock, breakthrough, elite, accelerate, dominate |
| **Belonging** | Join, community, insider, members, together, we |
| **Relief** | Finally, simple, easy, done, solved, no more |

### Words to Cut

| Remove | Replace with |
|--------|-------------|
| "Very" / "Really" | Stronger adjective or nothing |
| "Leverage" | "Use" |
| "Streamline" | Say exactly what it does |
| "Best-in-class" | Prove it with a specific |
| "Cutting-edge" | Describe the actual innovation |
| "Innovative" / "Revolutionary" | Say what's actually new |
| "Synergy" | Delete |
| "I think" / "I believe" | State it directly |

## Step 7: Landing Page Structure

**One page = one goal = one CTA (repeated 3+ times).**

### Minimal Effective Structure (most pages)

1. **Hero** — Headline + subhead + CTA + visual. Nothing else above the fold.
2. **Problem** — 3 pain points. Empathy first.
3. **Solution** — How it works (3 steps max). Simple.
4. **Benefits** — Feature → Benefit → "So What" for your top 3-5 features.
5. **Social proof** — Testimonials, logos, numbers. Match hierarchy above.
6. **Objection handling** — FAQ addressing your top 3 real objections.
7. **Final CTA** — Restate value prop + urgency + risk reversal.

### Industry-Specific Structure Adjustments

**Tech products:**
- Add: Integration logos near features ("Works with your existing tools")
- Add: Security/compliance badges near CTA
- Add: Comparison table vs top alternative (be honest, win on your strengths)
- Move: Pricing link into navbar (tech buyers want to price-check early)

**Entertainment:**
- Lead with: Emotion and identity (who this is for, what it feels like)
- Add: Social proof of the experience, not just results ("10k members watching together every Sunday")
- Use: Video or GIFs in hero — show the experience, don't describe it
- Minimise: Feature lists. Emphasise feeling.

**Motivation/self-improvement:**
- Lead with: The transformation (BAB framework in hero)
- Add: "This is NOT for you if..." — pre-qualifies and creates exclusivity
- Add: The method (people need to believe your approach is different)
- Use: Story-driven testimonials with before/after specifics
- Add: Guarantee front and centre — reduces risk anxiety

### Conversion Benchmarks

| Traffic source | Good | Great |
|---------------|------|-------|
| Cold ads | 3-5% | 8%+ |
| Email list | 10-15% | 25%+ |
| Organic/SEO | 5-8% | 12%+ |
| Referral | 8-12% | 20%+ |

## Step 8: CTA Rules

| Rule | Wrong | Right |
|------|-------|-------|
| Start with a verb | "Free Trial" | "Start my free trial" |
| First person | "Start your trial" | "Start my trial" |
| Reduce friction | "Buy now" | "Try free for 14 days" |
| Match awareness | "Learn more" (ready to buy) | "See pricing" (ready to buy) |
| Benefit over action | "Sign up" | "Get instant access" |

**Minimum 3 CTAs on any page longer than 2 screens.**
Place after: hero, each major section, social proof block, after objection handling, at the very bottom.

## Step 10: Editing

### 8-Point Copy Health Check (`/copy-check`)

| # | Check | Pass? |
|---|-------|-------|
| 1 | Does the headline pass the "so what?" test? | |
| 2 | Is the reader's #1 objection addressed? | |
| 3 | Can a 12-year-old understand it? | |
| 4 | Is there ONE clear CTA per section? | |
| 5 | Does it lead with benefit, not feature? | |
| 6 | Would you stop scrolling for this? | |
| 7 | Is every sentence earning its place? | |
| 8 | Does it sound like a human wrote it? | |

Score: X/8. Below 6 = rewrite before publishing.

### AI Copy Detection — Signs to Fix

- Starts with "In today's fast-paced world..."
- Uses "leverage", "streamline", "revolutionize"
- Lists exactly 3 things every single time
- Em-dash overuse
- "It's important to note that..."
- Perfect parallel structure in every list
- No contractions

**Fix:** Read it aloud. Would a smart friend say this in a conversation? If not, rewrite.

### Readability Targets

| Medium | Reading level | Sentence length |
|--------|--------------|----------------|
| Landing page | 7th-8th grade | 12-18 words |
| Social media | 5th grade | 8-12 words |
| Email | 6th-8th grade | 10-15 words |

### Words to Cut

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| "We are in the process of building" | "We're building" |
| "There are many reasons why" | "Here's why" |
| "It is important to note that" | [delete] |
| "Due to the fact that" | "Because" |
| "At this point in time" | "Now" |
| "A total of 47 customers" | "47 customers" |

## Step 11: Anti-Patterns (Never Do These)

**The Hype Machine** — "Revolutionary!", "Game-changing!", "Best ever!"
→ Replace hype with proof. "Saves 4 hours/week" > "Amazing results"

**Benefit-Less Copy** — Listing features without outcomes
→ Always ask "so what?" after every feature. People buy outcomes.

**Fake Urgency** — "Only 3 left!" on a digital product
→ Fake urgency destroys trust permanently. Only use urgency if it's real.

**The Kitchen Sink CTA** — 6 different CTAs at the bottom of the page
→ One primary CTA. One secondary at most. Confusion kills conversion.

**Testimonial Graveyard** — "Great product! — J.S."
→ Specific results + full name + title + company. No anonymity. No vague praise.

**Copycat Copy** — "The all-in-one platform for modern teams"
→ Find your specific angle. What do only YOU say? Lead with that.

**Question Flood** — 6 rhetorical questions in a row with no answers
→ Max one question per section. Always answer it immediately.

**Endless Scroll** — 15 sections on a simple landing page
→ Six sections is usually enough. Every section either helps conversion or hurts it.

**Assumption Army** — "As you know...", "Obviously...", "Clearly..."
→ State the problem directly. Let them recognise themselves. Don't condescend.

**Buzzword Bingo** — "Synergistic AI-powered cloud-native solution"
→ Say what it actually does. If you can't say it simply, you don't understand it.

**The We Problem** — Too much "we" and "our", not enough "you" and "your"
→ Ctrl+F "we/our" and flip them to "you/your" wherever possible.

## Step 12: Copy Scoring Rubric (`/score`)

Rate 0-5 on each dimension. Total × weight = score out of 100.

| Dimension | Weight | 0-2 (Weak) | 3-4 (Solid) | 5 (Exceptional) |
|-----------|--------|------------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Clarity** | 20% | Confusing, jargon | Clear to audience | Crystal clear to anyone |
| **Specificity** | 15% | Vague claims | Some numbers | Precise, verifiable |
| **Persuasion** | 15% | Lists features | Benefits present | "So What" chain throughout |
| **Voice** | 15% | Generic / AI | Consistent | Distinctive, memorable |
| **Structure** | 10% | Wall of text | Good flow | Perfect progressive disclosure |
| **CTA Strength** | 10% | "Submit" | Clear next step | Irresistible, friction removed |
| **Social Proof** | 10% | None | Present and relevant | Specific results from similar buyers |
| **Objections** | 5% | None addressed | Top one handled | All major objections pre-empted |

**85+ = publish. 70-84 = one more edit pass. Below 70 = significant rewrite.**

## Step 13: Swipe Templates

### Hero Section (AIDA)
```
[Headline — specific outcome or transformation]
[Subhead — who this is for + how it works in one sentence]
[Primary CTA — verb + benefit, first person]
[Secondary CTA — lower commitment option]
[Social proof micro-element — "X people / companies / reviews"]
```

### Testimonial Block
```
"[Specific result in their words]"
[Full Name], [Job Title], [Company]
[Optional: 1 sentence of context — "After [timeframe] of [using product]..."]
```

### FAQ (objection handling)
```
Q: [Exact objection, phrased as their question]
A: [Direct answer. Then proof. Then reassurance.]
```

### Feature → Benefit block
```
[Feature name]
[What it does — one sentence benefit]
[So What — emotional or practical outcome]
[Optional: proof point or testimonial snippet]
```
